Planning and Control

The Scrum process for the Flaties Project.

  • Project Proposal

    After our first client meeting we produced our project proposal we set out a plan to follow a Agile Scrum methodology. In the intial weeks we spent time planning and learning the technology stack, then the plan was to start a series of incremental sprints. Following a Scrum process the team would meet with the client to complete a sprint retrospective plan for the next sprint. However as time went along risks and issues occured which changed the flow and direction of the plan. Being Agile our team accomadated to these issues and adapted to make the nessary changes. As a R&D Team we set up a Trello board assigned tasks to complete our proposal and store our R&D artefacts like learning objectives

  • Asana Board

    At the start of our project we had the asana board set up to follow scrum and for the team to be sprinting. During the first semster we would meet with our client every 2-3 weeks as our sprint retrospective talk about what needed to be improved on, grooom the backlog, delegate ready to do tasks to members. As a team we were completing stand up meetings via facebook messenger and then also communicating progress to the client via slack. The rules of SCRUM where not followed stricitly by the team likely becuase working with a start up company "flying by the seat of your pants". In the second part of the project (semester 1) there was diffculty communicationg with the client and the team was 'lost' for the first two weeks of the semester. After finally meeting with the client all they wanted was a user testing report and no longer wanted any development to be done. However a week later we had a list of tasks to complete. Having the summer break upset the project plan and we lost track of what needed to be as our goals for the project seemed to be unclear. Even in the beginning of the project the specfic tasks where not set out clearly for the team as it seemed the client was not sure on our capablity of completing certain tasks. Orinally Casey was the main contact with the clients however in the second semster Latrell took over as he recieved first contact in the part two of the project. Lastly in the last month of our project we lost contact from our client and after trying to contact them we found they would not be working on the project until after we finsihed university. This threw a spanner in the works for the project plan.

  • Stand up Meetings

    At least once a week the team go through a "Stand up meeting" via messenger. Initialy we planned to do the stand up meetings every two days but dew to the nature of the project and R&D being 1 of 4 papers or scheduled around other commitments. We set the stand up meetings to one to two times per week. Some of the stand up meetings were unorthadox were others followed the set questions.

  • Sprint Retrospective and Sprint Planning

    When we would meet with the client we would complete a sprint retrospective verbally and then as an R&D team wrttien. We would also complete sprint planning in the planning process as a team we would decide what tasks needed to be worked on and move them from the product backlog to the ready to do sprint lane. We would set due dates for tasks although for some taks this method was not realistic. Using story points may have been a better option and play 'planning poker' to score each task. However planning poker is not part of scrum it is just an additional feature INSERT REF and the client did not follow this practice. Another artefact we did not use that is often used in scrum is a burndown or burnup chart. We did not use this because we did not put story points on the tasks. Also not all tasks had due dates, this then lead to the idea the team was more following a kanban methodolgy of constant flow of tasks.

Teamwork

At the beginning of this paper, we came up with a team agreement. In this team agreement, it basically contains all of the rules that the team has agreed on. It covers how the team will be developing, communicating, and the responsibility of each team member. Adding on, the team makes sures that every group meetings or discussions are held in a friendly matter. Each team member has the right to voice out their opinions and the team will listen to it. The team also makes sures that group meetings are held in a professional matter so that everyone can stay comfortable with each other. This agreement is signed by all the team members to show formalize the agreement.

When developing a task from Asana, we assign ourselves into the user stories that we are working on by adding our avatar underneath a task. This practice avoids confusion and lets a team member know if a task is available for developing or not. This is a good way of organizing and keeping our Asana Board nice and tidy.

We often do Pair Programming or Mob Programming when we get together as a team. We find this technique to be very useful as it allows us to learn from each other. In Pair or Mob Programming, the whole team communicates with each one another. We get to involve everyone in the team and get different inputs on how to solve a problem. This approach is a continous code review, therefore the team becomes more efficient as we get to code and reviews the code at the same time.

Communication

The team meets up once a week for a group discussion. The team finds it important to meet once a week because we are able to have face to face communication as a team. Through these face to face meetings, we are able to see the body language of everyone. This gives us live feedback from one another. We also found out that weekly meetings will help us build relationships, enhance trust, increase activity and increase effectiveness towards work that needs to be done.

Moreover, we also have been doing By-Daily Standup Meetings. This allows us the team to have a daily plan and keep everyone in the team updated. This method lets the team know if someone is stuck with a current task or not.

You can view our group meetings and it's details on our Trello board.

Furthermore, the team also has weekly meetings with the teamâ€™s supervisor, Jim Buchan. In the supervisor meetings, we update Jim on all of the work that has been done or needs to be done towards the project. Jim gives us feedback from our work and also gives us advice going forward. Supervisor meeting minutes can be viewed here.

Since the team cannot see each other a lot due to other commitments outside of the project, we have been using Facebook Messenger as our main communication tool. Facebook messenger has allowed us to keep everyone updated, ask questions about the project, set up meetings and just communicate with one another. Moreover, the team uses Slack as another tool to communicate with each other. However, we usually use Slack for when we are communicating with our client.

Tech Stack

For the front end of the stack Flaties uses HTML, CSS, Sass, ES6 and there is some jquery. At the start of the project it was mentioned we would start intergrating React in the frontend . In the second semster there was a disscusion of using Vue.js but the in the end went kept with what we were using. On the server side Flaties uses Node.js and express which then connects to mongoose to the mongoDB database. As a team we had to learn and understand these languages. At the beginning it took some time to understand the flow of how the data moved from the frontend to the backend via MVC model.

Packages and Toolkits

Installed packages to enhance workflow and the two database toolkits used.

Peer Review

Once a task completed it needs to be reviewed.

The purpose of doing a peer review is to reduce the amount of petenital bugs, less rework, better team communication and cohesiveness. Although a peer reivew can take time the benefits outweigh the cost on time.

On Asana the task is moved from 'In Progress' to 'Peer Review' Column. Over on GitLab a merge request is created for that feature to be merged to the develop branch.

Each change in code is reviewed and anything out of the ordinary is commented on to find the justification of why it was done.

If there are no required changes the merge request is accepted and the feature branch is merged to the develop branch. Over on Asana the task is moved to from 'Peer Review' to 'Done'
